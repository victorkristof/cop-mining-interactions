\r\n\t\tand adopted conclusions and/or draft decisions on many items, including \r\n\t\tseveral methodological issues, research and systematic observation, and \r\n\t\treducing emissions from deforestation in developing countries. However, \r\n\t\tdiscussions on the five-year programme of work on adaptation continued \r\n\t\tlate into Thursday night, and will be taken up in the SBSTA plenary on \r\n\t\tFriday. The <a href="#ad" style="text-decoration: none">AWG</a> \r\n\t\tconcluded its work very late on Thursday night after lengthy informal \r\n\t\tconsultations by agreeing on text outlining the \x93planning of future \r\n\t\twork.\x94</font></p>\r\n\t\t<p align="justify" class="ENB-Headline3"></p><p><i>\r\n\t\t<font face="Verdana" size="2"><a name="subsidiary"></a>SUBSIDIARY BODY \r\n\t\tFOR IMPLEMENTATION</font></i></p>\r\n\t\t<p align="justify" class="ENB-Body"><font face="Verdana" size="2"></font></p><p>\r\n\t\tADMINISTRATIVE, FINANCIAL AND INSTITUTIONAL MATTERS: </p>Delegates \r\n\t\tadopted SBI conclusions prepared by SBI Chair Becker on budget \r\n\t\tperformance for the biennium 2006-2007 (FCCC/SBI/2006/L.3) and on \r\n\t\tImplementation of the Headquarters Agreement (FCCC/SBI/2006/L.5).\r\n\t\t<p align="justify" class="ENB-Body"><font face="Verdana" size="2"></font></p><p>\r\n\t\tUNFCCC ARTICLE 4.8 &amp; 4.9: </p>On matters relating to the least developed \r\n\t\tcountries (LDCs), Samuel Adejuwon (Nigeria), who co-facilitated the \r\n\t\tinformal consultations, reported agreement on the LDC Expert Group\x92s \r\n\t\twork programme, and delegates adopted the conclusions (FCCC/SBI/2006/L.2).\r\n\t\t<p align="justify" class="ENB-Body"><font face="Verdana" size="2"></font></p><p>\r\n\t\tCAPACITY BUILDING (CONVENTION):</p> Contact group Co-Chair Crispin \r\n\t\tD\x92Auvergne (St. Lucia) summarized discussions. CHINA noted that the \r\n\t\tproposal by some parties to integrate monitoring with the comprehensive \r\n\t\treview would mean that the results of regular monitoring would only be \r\n\t\tseen every five years. Parties then adopted the conclusions \r\n\t\t(FCCC/SBI/2006/L.15).\r\n\t\t<p align="justify" class="ENB-Body"><font face="Verdana" size="2"></font></p><p>\r\n\t\tFINANCIAL MECHANISM (CONVENTION): </p>On the Special Climate Change \r\n\t\tFund, contact group chair Bubu Jallow (Gambia) reported progress on \r\n\t\tunderstanding what a staged approach to support various economic \r\n\t\tdevelopment issues could mean, noting that the SB 22 text would be the \r\n\t\tbasis of deliberations at SB 25. The G-77/CHINA said the phased approach \r\n\t\tis really only one stage. Delegates then adopted the conclusions (FCCC/SBI/2006/L.6).\r\n\t\t\r\n\t\t<p align="justify" class="ENB-Body"><font face="Verdana" size="2">On the \r\n\t\tthird review of the financial mechanism, contact group Co-Chair Marcia \r\n\t\tLevaggi (Argentina) noted that the annex to the draft SBI conclusions is \r\n\t\ta compilation of views expressed and could be used as the basis for \r\n\t\tnegotiations at SBI 25, but that it is still open for further input. \r\n\t\tParties then adopted the conclusions (FCCC/SBI/2006/L.4).</font></p>\r\n\t\t<p align="justify" class="ENB-Body"><font face="Verdana" size="2"></font></p><p>\r\n\t\tARRANGEMENTS OF INTERGOVERNMENTAL MEETINGS: </p>Parties adopted \r\n\t\tconclusions addressing arrangements for COP 12, COP/MOP 2 and future \r\n\t\tsessional periods, and review of arrangements for COP 11 and COP/MOP 1 (FCCC/SBI/2006/L.9).<p>\r\n\t\t</p>Contact group Co-Chair Sandea de Wet (South Africa) noted that the \r\n\t\tSBI, SBSTA, AWG and UNFCCC Dialogue will all take place in the next \r\n\t\tsessional period, and that meetings will close by 6:00 pm unless \r\n\t\t\x93exceptional circumstances\x94 arise.\r\n\t\t<p align="justify" class="ENB-Body"><font face="Verdana" size="2">\r\n\t\tRichard Kinley, Officer-in-Charge, UNFCCC Secretariat, said these \r\n\t\tconclusions on meeting arrangements were \x93quite revolutionary\x94 for this \r\n\t\tprocess. He said the Secretariat would discontinue the current practice \r\n\t\tof scheduling evening meetings, and that the decision on what \r\n\t\tconstitutes exceptional circumstances would be left to elected officers. \r\n\t\tHe noted the significant implications of restricting the negotiating \r\n\t\ttime available at meetings, given that there are about 36 substantive \r\n\t\titems or sub-items on the various agendas to be addressed in Nairobi. \r\n\t\tDescribing the changes as \x93shock therapy,\x94 he expressed his \x93deep \r\n\t\tanxiety\x94 that such an approach could lead to process \x93paralysis\x94 unless \r\n\t\tparties prioritize issues. </font></p>\r\n\t\t<p align="justify" class="ENB-Body"><font face="Verdana" size="2"></font></p><p>\r\n\t\tCAPACITY BUILDING (PROTOCOL):</p> Contact group Co-Chair Anders Turesson \r\n\t\t(Sweden) reported that there had been no resolution of this issue, and \r\n\t\tparties adopted conclusions that forward the matter to SBI 25 (FCCC/SBI/2006/L.16).\r\n\t\t\r\n\t\t<p align="justify" class="ENB-Body"><font face="Verdana" size="2"></font></p><p>\r\n\t\tCOMPLIANCE: </p>After debating and finally accepting a proposal by SAUDI \r\n\t\tARABIA to add that the SBI Chair will report orally to the COP/MOP 2 \r\n\t\t\x93based on the conclusions on this agenda item,\x94 delegates adopted the \r\n\t\tconclusions (FCCC/SBI/2006/L.1/Rev.1) as amended.\r\n\t\t<p align="justify" class="ENB-Body"><font face="Verdana" size="2"></font></p><p>\r\n\t\tINTERNATIONAL TRANSACTION LOG: </p>Delegates adopted the conclusions (FCCC/SBI/2006/L.8) \r\n\t\twithout comment. \r\n\t\t<p align="justify" class="ENB-Body"><font face="Verdana" size="2"></font></p><p>\r\n\t\tPRIVILEGES AND IMMUNITIES:</p> Chair Paul Watkinson (France) explained \r\n\t\tthat the Secretariat is requested to continue examining different \r\n\t\toptions for protecting individuals in constituted bodies under the \r\n\t\tProtocol. Delegates adopted the draft conclusions (FCCC/SBI/2006/L.10).\r\n\t\t<p align="justify" class="ENB-Body"><font face="Verdana" size="2"></font></p><p>\r\n\t\tNON-ANNEX I COMMUNICATIONS:</p> Delegates adopted conclusions on the \r\n\t\tnon-Annex I national communications consultative group of experts (FCCC/SBI/2006/L.12/Rev.1), \r\n\t\tand conclusions on compilation and synthesis of initial national \r\n\t\tcommunications (FCCC/SBI/2006/L.13/Rev.1) with a minor correction. On \r\n\t\tprovision of financial support, delegates adopted conclusions (FCCC/SBI/2006/L.7).\r\n\t\t<p align="justify" class="ENB-Body"><font face="Verdana" size="2"></font></p><p>\r\n\t\tBASE YEAR OF CROATIA:</p> Jim Penman (UK) reported on informal \r\n\t\tconsultations. Delegates discussed conclusions and a draft COP/MOP \r\n\t\tdecision on the level of Croatia\x92s base year emissions. Parties were \r\n\t\tunable to reach consensus on the draft texts and decided to refer the \r\n\t\tmatter to SB 25 (FCCC/SBI/2006/L.17.Rev.1).\r\n\t\t<p align="justify" class="ENB-Body"><font face="Verdana" size="2"></font></p><p>\r\n\t\tANNEX I COMMUNICATIONS:</p> On the synthesis of Annex I demonstrable \r\n\t\tprogress reports under Article 3.2 of the Protocol, SBI Chair Becker \r\n\t\treported on informal consultations. The RUSSIAN FEDERATION and UKRAINE, \r\n\t\topposed by the G-77/CHINA, proposed considering the matter at SB 26 \r\n\t\tinstead of SB 25.
